THE PEOPLE'S ARCHIVE OF RURAL INDIA: Software Engineer and Journalist

September 2016 - Present

The People's Archive of Rural India (PARI) is a spectacularly ambitious attempt to document "the everyday lives of everyday people" in India, and to subvert the mainstream media's disproportionate focus on urban centers. In the words of PARI's founder P. Sainath, the archive aims to be "a living archive of the past, a focused journal of the present, and a textbook for the future." I work on backend technologies at PARI's Mumbai headquarters, and I also travel through rural India writing articles and creating films.

TRAINING/FELLOWSHIPS

WILLIAM J. CLINTON FELLOW FOR SERVICE IN INDIA

September 2016 - June 2017

"Meeting India’s goals for sustainable development requires not only hard work and dedication, but also leaders with the firsthand experience and real-world skills to deliver practical, local solutions. The William J. Clinton Fellowship for Service in India is helping to shape the next generation of leaders committed to impactful change while also strengthening civil society to be more efficient and effective.

The Fellowship pairs a select number of highly skilled young professionals with leading NGOs and social enterprises in India in order to accelerate impact and create effective projects that are replicable, scalable, and sustainable. Through ten months of service and fieldwork, Fellows gain knowledge of development on the ground in the fields of education, livelihoods, public health, and social enterprise, honing and harnessing their growing skills as change agents capable of effecting lasting change. Together, Fellows and development sector leaders form dynamic partnerships to exchange knowledge and skills while sharing their passion and commitment to open new ways of looking at the world – ultimately transforming both the individual and organization." Sponsored by the American India Foundation.

Watch for monthly updates at the official fellowship blog, or follow my personal travel blog!
